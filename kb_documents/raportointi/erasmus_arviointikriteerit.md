# Erasmus+ Youth Final Report Quality Assessment Criteria

## Source: European Commission, Erasmus+ Programme
## Updated: 2024

---

## OVERVIEW

Erasmus+ projects are assessed using a standardized quality assessment framework. 
Projects are scored on a scale of 0-100 points, with specific thresholds:

| Score | Interpretation |
|-------|----------------|
| 75-100 | Very good to excellent - suitable for dissemination |
| 60-74 | Good - meets quality standards |
| 50-59 | Acceptable - minor issues |
| Below 50 | Poor - significant issues, may affect funding |

**Minimum threshold: 60 points overall AND at least half of maximum points per criterion**

---

## 1. QUALITY OF ACTIVITIES (Maximum: 25 points)

### 1.1 What is assessed:
- Were learning objectives achieved?
- Was the methodology appropriate for the target group?
- Were participants actively engaged?
- Was the activity well-organized?

### 1.2 Indicators of quality:
- Clear link between objectives, activities, and outcomes
- Evidence of active participation methods
- Appropriate duration and intensity
- Logical sequence of activities
- Adaptation to participant needs

### 1.3 Common weaknesses:
- Activities not aligned with objectives
- Passive learning methods (lectures only)
- Poor time management
- No adaptation to group needs
- Insufficient preparation

### 1.4 Scoring guide:
- 21-25 points: Excellent implementation, all objectives achieved
- 16-20 points: Good implementation, most objectives achieved
- 11-15 points: Adequate implementation, some objectives achieved
- 6-10 points: Partial implementation, few objectives achieved
- 0-5 points: Poor implementation, objectives not achieved

---

## 2. IMPACT AND DISSEMINATION (Maximum: 25 points)

### 2.1 What is assessed:
- What was the impact on participants?
- What was the impact on organisations?
- How were results disseminated?
- Are there lasting outcomes?

### 2.2 Types of impact:
- **Individual level**: Skills, attitudes, behaviour changes
- **Organisational level**: New practices, capacity building
- **Systemic level**: Policy influence, sector-wide changes
- **Community level**: Local benefits, social impact

### 2.3 Dissemination quality indicators:
- Variety of dissemination channels
- Appropriate target audiences
- Tangible outputs (publications, videos, toolkits)
- Evidence of reach (numbers, feedback)

### 2.4 Common weaknesses:
- No evidence of impact measurement
- Dissemination limited to social media
- No follow-up with participants
- Outputs not accessible or reusable

### 2.5 Scoring guide:
- 21-25 points: Strong, measurable impact; wide dissemination
- 16-20 points: Clear impact; good dissemination
- 11-15 points: Some impact visible; basic dissemination
- 6-10 points: Limited impact; minimal dissemination
- 0-5 points: No evidence of impact or dissemination

---

## 3. PROJECT MANAGEMENT (Maximum: 20 points)

### 3.1 What is assessed:
- Was the timeline followed?
- Was the budget used correctly?
- Were partners involved as planned?
- Was communication effective?

### 3.2 Quality indicators:
- Activities completed within timeframe
- Budget aligned with application (±15% tolerance)
- All partners actively contributed
- Clear roles and responsibilities
- Risk management in place

### 3.3 Budget compliance:
- Deviations over 15% require justification
- Transfer between budget headings must be explained
- Co-financing requirements must be met
- All expenditure must be documented

### 3.4 Common weaknesses:
- Significant delays without justification
- Budget overruns or significant underspending
- Partners not actively involved
- Poor documentation
- Communication breakdowns

### 3.5 Scoring guide:
- 17-20 points: Excellent management, full compliance
- 13-16 points: Good management, minor issues
- 9-12 points: Adequate management, some issues
- 5-8 points: Weak management, significant issues
- 0-4 points: Poor management, major non-compliance

---

## 4. EUROPEAN ADDED VALUE (Maximum: 15 points)

### 4.1 What is assessed:
- What was gained from international cooperation?
- How did cross-cultural exchange occur?
- Were EU priorities addressed?
- Was transnational collaboration meaningful?

### 4.2 EU priorities (2021-2027):
- Inclusion and diversity
- Digital transformation
- Environment and climate action
- Participation in democratic life

### 4.3 Quality indicators:
- Clear benefits from transnational cooperation
- Intercultural learning opportunities
- Results that couldn't be achieved nationally
- Contribution to EU policy objectives

### 4.4 Common weaknesses:
- International dimension only superficial
- No evidence of cultural exchange
- EU priorities mentioned but not addressed
- Collaboration could have been done nationally

### 4.5 Scoring guide:
- 13-15 points: Strong European dimension, addresses EU priorities
- 10-12 points: Clear European added value
- 7-9 points: Some European dimension visible
- 4-6 points: Limited European added value
- 0-3 points: No clear European dimension

---

## 5. SUSTAINABILITY AND FOLLOW-UP (Maximum: 15 points)

### 5.1 What is assessed:
- How will results be maintained?
- Are there follow-up activities planned?
- What resources exist for continuation?
- Is there potential for scaling?

### 5.2 Sustainability dimensions:
- **Institutional**: Results integrated into regular work
- **Financial**: Resources for continuation secured
- **Political**: Recognition and support from authorities
- **Technical**: Infrastructure for ongoing use

### 5.3 Quality indicators:
- Concrete follow-up plan
- Resources allocated for continuation
- Partnerships sustained
- Results integrated into practice

### 5.4 Common weaknesses:
- No sustainability plan
- Results exist only during project period
- No resources for continuation
- Partnership ends with project

### 5.5 Scoring guide:
- 13-15 points: Strong sustainability, clear continuation
- 10-12 points: Good prospects for sustainability
- 7-9 points: Some sustainability measures
- 4-6 points: Limited sustainability
- 0-3 points: No evidence of sustainability

---

## CONSEQUENCES OF ASSESSMENT

| Overall Score | Consequence |
|--------------|-------------|
| ≥75 points | Full grant, highlighted as good practice |
| 60-74 points | Full grant |
| 50-59 points | Grant may be reduced by up to 10% |
| 40-49 points | Grant may be reduced by up to 20% |
| <40 points | Grant may be reduced by up to 40% |
| <25 points | Grant may be fully recovered |

---

## ERASMUS+ YOUTH QUALITY STANDARDS

All mobility activities must comply with:

1. **Before the activity**:
   - Clear learning objectives defined
   - Participants properly prepared
   - Practical arrangements made

2. **During the activity**:
   - Non-formal learning methods used
   - Intercultural learning facilitated
   - Safe environment ensured

3. **After the activity**:
   - Learning outcomes recognized (Youthpass)
   - Follow-up support provided
   - Results disseminated
